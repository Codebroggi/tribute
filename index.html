Edmund Burke

politician, philosopher and writer

Edmund Burke was an Irish statesman as well as an author, orator, political theorist, and philosopher who, after moving to London, served as a member of parliament for many years in the House of Commons with the Whig Party. Burke was a strong proponent of maintaining solid moral virtues in society, and of the importance of religion in attaining that virtue and keeping society together.


Here are some information:
  • born in Dublin on 12 January 1729
  • educated at Trinity College, Dublin and then went to London to study law. Later he gave this up.
  • settled in London, concentrating on a literary and political career. He became a member of parliament in 1765.
  • The outbreak of the French Revolution in 1789 gave Burke his greatest target. He expressed his hostility in 'Reflections on the Revolution in France' (1790).
  • Burke retired from parliament in 1794.
  • died on 9 July 1797
